Kedarnath Temple

Kedarnath is a sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Uttarakhand at an altitude of 3,583 meters in the Himalayas. In Hindu tradition, it is believed that Lord Shiva manifested in the form of Jyotirlingam or the cosmic light. There are 12 such Jyotirlingas, and Kedarnath is the highest among them.

Badrinath Temple

Badrinath is one of the holiest Hindu shrines, dedicated to Lord Vishnu, located in Uttarakhand, India, at an altitude of 3,133 meters in the Garhwal Himalayas.The Badrinath Temple also known as the Badrinarayan Temple, located in Uttarakhand's Badrinath town, is one of the Char Dhams (four important pilgrimages) in the state. There are four pilgrim-destinations namely Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath, collectively known as Char Dham. These pilgrimage centres draw large number of pilgrims each year, thus becoming the most important hubs of religious travel in the whole of Northern India. Badrinath is located at an elevation of around 3,100 m. Located in the Garhwal Himalayas, on the banks of the Alaknanda river, this sacred town lies between Nar and Narayana mountain ranges.

Bake Bihari Temple

Banke Bihari is a famous temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, located in Vrindavan, Uttar Pradesh. It is one of the most revered temples in India and attracts millions of devotees.Shri Bankey Bihari Mandir is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, in the holy city of Vrindavan in the Mathura district of Uttar Pradesh. It is situated near Shri RadhaVallabh Temple.This temple is among the 7 temples of Thakur of Vrindavan including Shri RadhaVallabh Ji, Shri Govind Dev Ji and four others. Bankey Bihari Ji was originally worshipped at Nidhivana.Bankey means “bent in three places” and Bihari means “supreme enjoyer.” The image of Lord Krishna stands in the Tribhanga posture. Haridas Swami originally worshipped this devotional image under the name of Kunj-Bihari (“Enjoyer of Lakes”). ‘Bānke’ means ‘bent’, and ‘Bihāri’ or ‘Vihāri’ means ‘enjoyer’. This is how Kṛṣṇa, who is bent in three places, got the name “Bānke Bihāri”.

Prem Mandir

Prem Mandir is a beautiful and modern temple located in Vrindavan, Uttar Pradesh. Dedicated to Lord Krishna and Radha, it is known for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and spiritual ambiance. Prem Mandir is a Hindu temple in Vrindavan, Mathura, India. It is maintained by Jagadguru Kripalu Parishat, an international non-profit, educational, spiritual, charitable trust. The complex is on a 54-acre site on the outskirts of Vrindavan, and is dedicated to Lord Radha Krishna and Sita Ram. The temple structure was established by the fifth Jagadguru, Kripalu Maharaj. Figures of Shri Krishna and his followers depicting important events surrounding the Lord’s existence cover the main temple.

Akshar Dhaam Temple

Akshardham is a grand Hindu temple located in Delhi, India, dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan. It was inaugurated in 2005 and is known for its stunning architecture, cultural exhibitions, and spiritual significance. Located in the heart of India i.e., New Delhi, Akshardham Temple is a marvel of architecture that unfolds the cultural legacy of thousands of years and is a tribute to Lord Swaminarayan (1781- 1830) who is the avatars, devas and great sages of Hinduism. The construction of the grand structure took almost 5 years and was inaugurated on 6 November, 2005. Today the imposing structure is standing on the banks of the Yamuna River and attracts millions of tourists and pilgrims across the globe.

Mahakaleshwar Temple

Mahakaleshwar is a sacred temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and holds immense religious significance for Hindus. Shri Mahakaleshwar of Ujjayini is known among the twelve celebrated Jyotirlingas in India. The glory of Mahakaleshwar temple has been vividly described in various Purans. Starting with Kalidasa, many sanskrit poets have eulogized this temple in emotive terms. Ujjain used to be the central point for calculation of Indian time and Mahakala was considered as the distinctive presiding deity of Ujjain.The presiding deity of time, Shiva, in all his splendor, reigns eternal in Ujjain.

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Kashi Vishwanath is one of the most revered temples in India, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, it is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and holds immense spiritual significance for Hindus. Kashi Vishwanath Temple, temple located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is among the most important Hindu temples in India and is one of the 12 temples where Shiva is believed to have appeared as a column of light. The Kashi Vishwanath Temple stands on the west bank of the Ganges River. It is dedicated to Shiva, who has been worshipped here for hundreds of years as Vishwanatha or Vishweshwara, “Lord of the Universe.”

Banaras Ghaat

Banaras Ghats are a series of steps leading down to the River Ganges in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, one of the oldest and holiest cities in India. There are over 80 ghats in the city, each with its own significance and history. Banaras Ghat, or more commonly known as Varanasi Ghats, are a series of steps leading down to the River Ganges (Ganga) in the city of Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh. Varanasi is one of the oldest cities in the world and is considered the spiritual capital of India. The ghats are essential to the culture, history, and daily rituals of the city.
